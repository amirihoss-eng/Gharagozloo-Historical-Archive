## Archival summary

Son of Mohammad Hossein Khan; senior Gharagozloo commander killed after being severely wounded in the Herat campaign.

## Chronology and documented activities

- **1255 AH** — Gharagozloo participation in Mohammad Shah’s Herat campaign — Herat: Thousands of Gharagozloo troops served under Nabi Khan. Abdollah Khan supported his force; Gharagozloo troops helped assault the fortifications. Nabi Khan was killed after severe wounds. [`E0002`, confirmed]

## Evidence-backed claims

- Nabi Khan was a son of Mohammad Hossein Khan Gharagozloo. **Confidence:** confirmed. Book pages 60. [`C0016`]
- Nabi Khan commanded thousands of Gharagozloo troops in Mohammad Shah’s Herat campaign. **Confidence:** confirmed. Book pages 119. [`C0017`]
- Nabi Khan died shortly after being removed from the battlefield with severe wounds. **Confidence:** confirmed. Book pages 120. [`C0023`]
- Nabi Khan was transferred with other Gharagozloo dead for burial in Mashhad by order of Mohammad Shah. **Confidence:** confirmed. Book pages 121. [`C0024`]
