## Chronology and documented activities

- **12 Rabi I 1311 AH** — Hosam al-Molk appointed governor of Kermanshah — Kermanshah: After extensive attempts to obtain provincial office, Zeyn al-Abedin Khan Hosam al-Molk was appointed governor of Kermanshah. [`E0009`, confirmed]
- **1309 AH** — Marriage of Gholamreza Khan and Qamar al-Saltaneh — Kermanshah: A lavish dynastic marriage arranged by Zeyn al-Abedin Khan Hosam al-Molk. [`E0007`, confirmed]
- **1310 AH** — Arrest of Hamadan Gharagozloo amir-tumans — Hamadan: Following unrest in Hamadan, Hosam al-Molk, Saed al-Saltaneh, and Zia al-Molk were dismissed and detained. [`E0008`, confirmed]
- **1322 AH** — Foundation of the Islamiyyeh School at Shervin — Shervin: Hosam al-Molk and Zia al-Molk established the Islamiyyeh School. [`E0018`, confirmed]
- **1324 AH** — Gharagozloo grain blockade and Hamadan bread crisis — Hamadan: Gharagozloo landowners restricted grain deliveries during conflict with the Majles-e Favaed-e Ammeh and Constitutionalists. [`E0019`, confirmed]
- **1326 AH** — Gharagozloo regiments against Tabriz Constitutionalists — Tabriz: Regiments commanded by Sardar Akram, Mansur al-Dowleh, and Amir Afkham supported Mohammad Ali Shah against Tabriz. [`E0021`, confirmed]
- **1329-1330 AH** — Gharagozloo participation in the Salar al-Dowleh crisis: Amir Nezam and other khans shifted positions during Salar al-Dowleh’s rebellion; Amir Afkham later joined government forces. [`E0022`, confirmed]
- **1334 AH** — Russian occupation of Hamadan and appointment of Amir Afkham — Hamadan: Russian forces occupied Hamadan with assistance from Amir Afkham, who was installed as governor. [`E0024`, confirmed]
- **25 Shawwal 1326 AH** — Killing of Seyyed Jamal al-Din Vaez Esfahani — Borujerd: The preacher was delivered to Amir Afkham’s circle and killed in Borujerd. [`E0020`, confirmed]

## Evidence-backed claims

- Zeyn al-Abedin Khan arranged the marriage of his son Gholamreza Khan to Qamar al-Saltaneh in 1309 AH. **Confidence:** confirmed. Book pages 139. [`C0038`]
- Hosam al-Molk offered 100,000 tomans for the governments of Hamadan, Borujerd, and Luristan, then 80,000 tomans to the shah and 20,000 to Amin al-Soltan for Kerman. **Confidence:** confirmed. Book pages 141. [`C0041`]
- Hosam al-Molk was appointed governor of Kermanshah on 12 Rabi I 1311 AH. **Confidence:** confirmed. Book pages 141. [`C0042`]
- Amir Afkham was a leading royalist Qajar commander against Constitutionalists. **Confidence:** confirmed. Book pages 159-161. [`C0066`]
- The book attributes the killing of Seyyed Jamal al-Din Vaez Esfahani to Amir Afkham’s order. **Confidence:** confirmed. Book pages 159-161. [`C0067`]
- Amir Afkham assisted Russian forces in occupying Hamadan and became governor under Russian protection. **Confidence:** confirmed. Book pages 164-166. [`C0073`]
