## Chronology and documented activities

- **1269-1272 AH** — Mahmoud Khan mission to Russia: Mahmoud Khan served as Iranian envoy to Russia and returned with promotion and royal honors. [`E0013`, confirmed]
- **1275 AH** — Mahmoud Khan steam spinning factory project: Mahmoud Khan imported machinery from Moscow for a modern steam-powered spinning factory; the project failed for lack of trained operators. [`E0014`, confirmed]
- **1277 AH** — Mahmoud Khan appointed first minister of commerce: Mahmoud Khan Naser al-Molk became the first holder of the newly established ministry of commerce. [`E0015`, confirmed]
- **1278-1307 AH** — Deaths of major Gharagozloo khans: The book records the deaths of Abdollah Khan Sarem al-Saltaneh, Ali Naqi Khan, Ali Khan Nosrat al-Molk, Amanollah Khan, Mostafa Qoli Khan, Mahmoud Khan, and Mohammad Hossein Khan Hosam al-Molk. [`E0016`, confirmed]
- **Shaban 1254 AH** — Mahmoud Khan appointed colonel and successor to Nabi Khan’s command: Mohammad Shah appointed Mahmoud Khan colonel and placed Nabi Khan’s former troops under his command. [`E0012`, confirmed]

## Evidence-backed claims

- Mahmoud Khan was the son of Nabi Khan and a descendant of Mohammad Hossein Khan Ashiqloo. **Confidence:** confirmed. Book pages 128. [`C0025`]
- Mahmoud Khan Gharagozloo argued for peace in a state consultation during the Anglo-Persian crisis. **Confidence:** probable. Book pages 135. [`C0029`]
- Mahmoud Khan was the son of Nabi Khan and a descendant of Mohammad Hossein Khan of the Ashiqloo branch. **Confidence:** confirmed. Book pages 142-143. [`C0046`]
- After Nabi Khan’s death in the Herat campaign, Mahmoud Khan was placed in command of his father’s troops. **Confidence:** confirmed. Book pages 142-143. [`C0047`]
- Mahmoud Khan served as deputy governor of Hamadan and the Gharagozloo districts during the late reign of Fath Ali Shah. **Confidence:** confirmed. Book pages 142-143. [`C0048`]
- Mahmoud Khan received authority over Darjazin, Jahraman, Tohidlu, and the districts attached to Khan Baba Khan in 1246-1247 AH. **Confidence:** confirmed. Book pages 142-143. [`C0049`]
- Mahmoud Khan was appointed colonel in 1254 AH and promoted to sartip in 1255 AH. **Confidence:** confirmed. Book pages 143. [`C0050`]
- Mahmoud Khan was appointed Iranian envoy to Russia in 1269 AH. **Confidence:** confirmed. Book pages 144-145. [`C0051`]
- After returning from Saint Petersburg in 1272 AH, Mahmoud Khan was promoted to mirpanj and received a royal robe. **Confidence:** confirmed. Book pages 144-145. [`C0052`]
- Mahmoud Khan later held senior posts including foreign affairs, war, state buildings, and government papers administration. **Confidence:** confirmed. Book pages 144-145. [`C0053`]
- Mahmoud Khan imported approximately 90,000 tomans of machinery from Moscow for a steam-powered spinning factory, but the project failed because trained personnel were unavailable. **Confidence:** confirmed. Book pages 146-147. [`C0054`]
- Mahmoud Khan became the first minister of commerce when that ministry was established in 1277 AH. **Confidence:** confirmed. Book pages 146-147. [`C0055`]
