## Chronology and documented activities

- **1299 SH** — Arrest and release of Amir Nezam after the 1921 coup: Amir Nezam was arrested by Seyyed Zia’s government and reportedly paid 25,000 tomans to secure release. [`E0025`, confirmed]
- **1314 AH** — Abdollah Khan appointed governor of Khuzestan — Khuzestan: Abdollah Khan Saed al-Saltaneh was appointed governor and received the title Sardar Akram. [`E0017`, confirmed]
- **1326 AH** — Gharagozloo regiments against Tabriz Constitutionalists — Tabriz: Regiments commanded by Sardar Akram, Mansur al-Dowleh, and Amir Afkham supported Mohammad Ali Shah against Tabriz. [`E0021`, confirmed]
- **1329-1330 AH** — Gharagozloo participation in the Salar al-Dowleh crisis: Amir Nezam and other khans shifted positions during Salar al-Dowleh’s rebellion; Amir Afkham later joined government forces. [`E0022`, confirmed]
- **21 Shawwal 1311 AH** — Gharagozloo regiments sent against unrest in Shiraz: Gharagozloo regiments under Saed al-Saltaneh were sent to suppress unrest in Shiraz; seven or eight people were killed. [`E0011`, confirmed]
- **before 1316 AH** — Abdollah Khan appointed governor of Khuzestan; titles granted to sons — Khuzestan: The shah appointed Abdollah Khan governor of Khuzestan and titled him Sardar Akram. At his request Hossein Qoli Khan received Sa'ed al-Saltaneh and Mohtaj Ali Khan received Ejlal al-Mamalek. [`E0001`, confirmed]
