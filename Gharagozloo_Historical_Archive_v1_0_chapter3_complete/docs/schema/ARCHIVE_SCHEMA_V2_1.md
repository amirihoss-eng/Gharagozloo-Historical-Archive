# Archive Schema v2.1 — Evidence Classification

Version 2.1 adds explicit source-critical classification for claims.

## New tables

### `evidence_types`
Controlled vocabulary for the nature of evidence or interpretation.

### `claim_evidence_profiles`
Links a claim to:
- evidence type;
- assertion scope;
- the source/author's position;
- an assessment note.

This allows the archive to distinguish a contemporary report, a narrative fact,
an author's synthesis, a scholarly opinion, an unresolved inference, and a rejected
hypothesis without flattening them into equivalent facts.

## Chapter 1 policy

Chapter 1 contains competing theories of origin and discontinuous early named figures.
The archive therefore:
- records named people and events when the text is explicit;
- classifies origin conclusions as synthesis or opinion;
- preserves rejected theories as historiography;
- does not construct a continuous family pedigree from Seljuk or Timurid names to the
  later Hamadan families.
